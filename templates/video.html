Video 1 Summary
This video, provides a detailed walkthrough on how to correctly complete Ethics Form A for research involving human participants. It is a crucial resource for students preparing to apply for ethics clearance as part of their capstone or postgraduate research projects. The video explains each section of the form, including consent procedures, risk classification, research methodology alignment, data confidentiality, and how to handle vulnerable populations. The video offers practical advice, common pitfalls to avoid, and answers key student questions, making the video an essential guide for preparing a successful ethics application. Students are encouraged to watch the full video to ensure they meet all ethical requirements before submitting their forms.



Video 2 Summary
This follow-up video, provides a detailed explanation of Ethics Form B, which is required when using secondary data that is not publicly available and involves no direct interaction with human participants. The session distinguishes Form B from Form A, and provides a real anonymized example to demonstrate how to correctly complete the application. The video also explains how to request data access from institutions, address data that contains identifiers, anonymize sensitive information, and manage risks. Key insights include how to handle delayed permission letters, the importance of ethical consistency, and the difference between permission letters and data waivers. This video is essential for students conducting desk-based research using restricted secondary datasets and ensures their ethics applications align with university and legal standards.
